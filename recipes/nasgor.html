Nasi Goreng

Wait, Isn't That Just Fried Rice? Always Have Been

Nasi goreng is Indonesian fried rice, I mean it literally means fried rice in Bahasa Indonesia. What's the difference you might ask? Well, nasi goreng uses sweet soy sauce in its ingredients. Sweet soy sauce is pretty much a staple in nasi goreng, a soy sauce that's native to Indonesia. Sweet soy sauce's consistency is thicker than a regula soy sauce. So basically nasi goreng is just a fried rice mixed with sweet soy sauce, THAT'S IT!!

Generally speaking, fried rice is very customizable. You can have variety of different toppings. But for this recipe's sake, we'll use shrip and egg as the main topping, feel free to add whatever you like! Nasi goreng gives you a sense of adventure whenever you try different toppings. We recommend you to experiment with your dish.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of leftover rice if possibel, newly cooked rice is fine
  • 3 tbsp of sweet soy sauce
  • 150 g of clean shrimps
  • 2-3 eggs (you can add sunny side fried egg for individual serving)
  • 1 diced onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp red chilli
  • 1 chopped shallot
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 tsp shrimp paste (for immersive experience, but optional)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Heat the oil over high heat in a large skillet or wok (preferably wok since we need that asian authenticity).
  2. Add chilli and garlic, stir for 10 seconds.
  3. Add onion and shallot, cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add shrimps, cook until they mostly turn pinkish-white, then add 1 tbsp sweet soy sauce and cook until shrimps are mostly cooked through and a bit caramelised.
  5. Add 2 eggs and scramble them nicely. This should only take 10 seconds
  6. Add rice, 2 tbsp sweet soy sauce, oyster sauce, and shrimp paste, if using. Stir constantly, for 2 minutes until sauce reduces down and rice grains start to caramelise. Then add salt according to your taste, mix well so the salt will spread evenly.
  7. Turn off the heat and serve the dish in a plate
  8. Cook a sunny side fried egg with runny yolk (yum!!) to add on top of the serving. This is optional but a must if you want the authentic nasi goreng experience.
  9. For garnish you can add sliced tomatoes and cucumber, saut√©ed bok choy, or what ever veggies you like. Also add indonesian fish or shrimp cracker if available.
  10. Don't just stare at it, EAT IT!! Don't forget to stab the egg yolk first hehe.

Feel free to try different variations. This is fried rice after all, the dish is your playground. For example, you can use sausage instead of shrimp, or you can add chicken, and so on. It feels like there are flavors that are waiting to be discovered, just like hunting for treasures, so have fun!
