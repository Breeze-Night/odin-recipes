Salmon Mentai Rice

Mentai? Sounds Awfully a Lot Like the Word That Starts with H

Basically, this dish is just baked salmon and rice with mentai sauce. Mentai or mentaiko (明太子) is seasoned pollack roe, the roe of Alaska pollack (roe is fish eggs in case you don't know). There are different variations of mentaiko. You can see mentaiko sold in different colors that range from pink to bright red, the redder it gets, the spicier it is.

This dish will capture the taste of the ocean in your tiny little mouth. When eating this, it will feel like as if you are swimming with a school of fish exploring the vast sea. Do you want that experience? You can make it in the comfort of your own kitchen!!

Ingredients

Seasoning for rice

  • 2 small portions of white rice
  • 2 tbsp of nori powder
  • 1 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 1 tsp of sesame oil

Seasoning for salmon

  • 130 g of salmon
  • 2 tbsp of soy sauce, smeared onto salmon

Mentai sauce

  • 200 ml of Japanese mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp of mentaiko
  • 1 tbsp of tomato sauce
  • 1 tsp of chili sauce
  • Chili powder taste
  • Cheddar cheese (optional)

Steps

  1. First, we make the butter sauce. Prepare a bowl, pour all the ingredients for the sauce into it. Mix well.
  2. Season the rice with nori powder, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Mix well.
  3. Transfer the rice to a heat-resistant dish or you can also use aluminum foil packaging. Spread the rice, set aside.
  4. Now it's time to process salmon. Separate salmon meat and skin. Then cut the salmon into thin slices to size according to taste.
  5. Cover the salmon with soy sauce until smooth, then arrange the salmon into a baking pan. Bake briefly using a blow torch until the surface changes color.
  6. Arrange the salmon on the rice surface, pour the mentai sauce over the salmon until it is even and add cheddar cheese if you want that cheesy sensation. Covers the entire surface.
  7. Bake the surface of the mentai sauce with a blow torch until it turns brown. If you don't have a blow tourch you can bake it in the oven for 20 minutes in 180°C. Add nori powder or parsley on top as garnish.
  8. Pat yourself on the back and enjoy the food, Itadakimasu!

The beauty of this dish is that you can make variations how ever you like!! Instead of using salmon, you can use beef, chicken, crab, or even make it vegan by replacing the fish roe with seaweed caviar, the choice is yours!
